# What success looks like

  - There's lots of funding available (via LazyGiving) for social-media creators who write noninflammatory content that deals with "the other side" fairly and respectfully. Good content is noticed quickly, submitted to the Civility AI attester, and shown to more donors. Scouts can fund promising creators early, then recover those contributions at cost when the wider community donates after seeing successful work; the scouts' public records help them attract delegated budgets. Other donors can simply delegate to someone they trust or wait for results and donate to close an outstanding reimbursement loop.
  - There are some very-reasonable common-ground statements on Tally (much more reasonable than the extreme viewpoints being pushed by the media), with some big numbers next to them; those big numbers on the common-ground statements are to some extent direct signers, but there are also big numbers coming via implication arrows from very-reasonable left-leaning or right-leaning versions of those statements.
  - There are also big Tally numbers, and big LazyGiving numbers, for meta-statements like the "I think most people who currently identify as left-wing or right-wing are basically normal sane people with common-sense views who have more in common with each other than our extremely-polarized political/cultural environment implies, and I want to see this quiet middle majority recognize itself as a majority and gain more influence." On Alignment, statements like that show lots of projects aligned toward that cause, and there's lots of funding for them.
