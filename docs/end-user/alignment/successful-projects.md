# Successful projects

There are two different things you might believe about a project, and Alignment lets you say each one separately:

- **It's aligned with a cause** — you think the project is *trying* to help. ([vouch for alignment](help-connect-things.md))
- **It succeeded at a cause** — you think it *actually delivered* the value it set out to.

Same project, same cause, two different claims. "Aligned" is a bet on intent; "successful" is a verdict on results. A project can be aligned for a long time before anyone is willing to say it succeeded — and that second vouch is the one that retroactive funders are waiting for.

## The Successful projects page

Every cause board has a **Successful** view alongside its usual list of aligned projects. It shows projects that

1. people in your trust network have **vouched as successful** at this cause, and
2. still have **donation receipts outstanding** — i.e. early backers who haven't been bought out yet.

That second filter is the whole point. This isn't a hall of fame; it's a **to-do list**. A project that delivered *and* whose scouts have already been fully repaid drops off the page — there's nothing left for a retroactive funder to do there.

### It's not an official "this succeeded" stamp

There is no committee here and no official pronouncement. The page is an **aggregate of other people's vouches**, filtered through your trust network — exactly like the aligned-projects list. You don't have to agree that anything on it actually delivered; you're looking at what *the people you trust* think delivered. If the list looks wrong, the fix is to change who you trust, or widen the discovery slider. If you've looked at a project someone vouched for and you don't think it produced real value — you can say so, by clicking the Downtrust button (decreasing your trust in whoever vouched for it).

This is the same mechanism that backs alignment vouches (you can [Downtrust](help-connect-things.md#disagree-downtrust) a project you think isn't even *trying* to serve the cause) — applied here to the stronger "it delivered" claim. That's the real consequence of a success vouch: not money lost, but **reputation spent**. A voucher whose "it delivered!" calls don't hold up loses standing with the people who disagree, and their future vouches reach fewer people.

## Who this page is for

Some people enjoy hunting for diamonds in the rough — backing projects early, before anyone's sure, and sorting the real ones from the scams themselves. If that's you, great: be a [scout](../lazyGiving/fund-something.md), fund early, and retroactive funders will buy you out later.

But plenty of people want to support a cause **without** signing up to predict winners or sniff out fraud in advance. If that's you, the Successful projects page is your main surface. The move is simple:

> Find projects you agree have done genuine good, and **buy and burn their outstanding donation receipts** until the number reaches zero.

When you buy and burn a receipt, your money goes to an early backer who took the risk when the project was still a gamble, and the receipt is permanently retired. Drive the outstanding count to zero and every scout who bet early on that project has been paid back — at a price set by people who *later* agreed it was worth doing. You never had to guess; you just had to recognize good work after the fact, which is far easier. (The full story is in [retroactive funding](../lazyGiving/retroactive-funding.md).)

### What each card shows

- **What it accomplished** — the delivered result, not just the original pitch.
- **Who vouched it succeeded** — trust-filtered to your network.
- **Outstanding receipts** — the number you can drive toward zero.
- **Current receipt price** — what buying one out costs right now.
- **Buy & burn** — the call to action. The purchase and burn happen on [LazyGiving](../lazyGiving/index.md), where the project and its receipts live; this page is just where you discover what's worth doing.

Projects are sorted by **how deserving-but-unpaid** they are — roughly, how confident your network is that it succeeded, times how many receipts are still outstanding — so the most proven, least-repaid work floats to the top.

## How to vouch that a project succeeded

This is the mirror image of [vouching for alignment](help-connect-things.md), and it lives in the same place — on Alignment, because a success vouch is anchored to a **cause**, and causes are an Alignment concept. (LazyGiving doesn't know about causes; it just knows about individual projects.)

1. Open the cause the project delivered value for — or a more specific statement that [implies](how-alignment-works.md) it. ("Cleaned the Anacostia" implies "clean rivers," so a success vouch on the specific statement also surfaces on the broader cause's Successful view.)
2. Find the project and choose **vouch successful** (you can also reach this from the project's page on LazyGiving, which deep-links you back here so you can pick the cause).
3. Submit. Like an alignment vouch, it's free, public, permanent, and part of your reputation. The thing you're putting at stake is exactly that reputation: if people look at the project and disagree that it delivered, they can downgrade their trust in you (see ["I don't agree this delivered"](#its-not-an-official-this-succeeded-stamp) above), and your future vouches will reach fewer people. So vouch for success only when you'd stand behind the result.

Anyone in your followers' trust networks will then see the project on their Successful view for that cause.
