# Successful projects

There are two different things you might believe about a project, and Alignment lets you say each separately:

- **It's aligned with a cause** — the project is trying to help. ([vouch for alignment](help-connect-things.md))
- **It succeeded at a cause** — the project actually delivered value.

A project can be aligned long before anyone is willing to say it succeeded. That second vouch is what retroactive donors are waiting for.

## The Successful projects page

Every cause board has a **Successful** view. It shows projects that

1. people in your trust network have **vouched as successful** at this cause, and
2. still have **reimbursement outstanding** for early contributors.

This is a to-do list, not a hall of fame. Once a project's retained reimbursement claims have all been funded, there is nothing left for a retroactive donor to do and it drops from the list.

### It's not an official stamp

The page aggregates other people's vouches through your trust network. You do not have to agree that a listed project delivered. If a success vouch looks wrong, you can Downtrust the person who made it, just as you can for an alignment vouch. The consequence of a bad vouch is reputational: fewer people will rely on that person's future judgment.

## Who this page is for

Some people enjoy finding promising work early. If that is you, [fund as a scout](../lazyGiving/fund-something.md). Your contribution may later be reimbursed at cost, and your track record can help you earn larger delegated budgets.

If you prefer to see results first, this page is your main surface:

> Find a project you agree has done genuine good, then donate to bring its outstanding reimbursement toward zero.

Your donation enters the project's reimbursement waterfall. It makes funds available to early contributors pro-rata, and nobody can receive more than they originally contributed. Recognition receipts remain non-transferable. The goal is to get early contributors' money back so they can fund the next project, not to provide a financial return.

### What each card shows

- **What it accomplished** — the delivered result, not just the original pitch.
- **Who vouched it succeeded** — filtered through your trust network.
- **Outstanding reimbursement** — the amount early contributors can still recover.
- **Close the loop** — the donation action on the project's LazyGiving page.

Projects prioritize proven work that still has substantial reimbursement outstanding.

## How to vouch that a project succeeded

A success vouch is anchored to a cause, so it lives on Alignment:

1. Open the cause the project served, or a more specific statement that implies it.
2. Find the project and choose **vouch successful**. A LazyGiving project can deep-link you here.
3. Submit the public vouch. Put your reputation behind only results you would defend.

Anyone whose trust network includes you can then see the project in the Successful view for that cause.
