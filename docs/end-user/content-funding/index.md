# Content Funding

Content Funding is [LazyGiving](../lazyGiving/index.md), pointed at social-media content.

On LazyGiving, a creator describes a project, supporters pledge, and the money moves only if it reaches a funding goal — otherwise everyone is refunded. Content Funding applies that same mechanism to the things creators already make: tweets and threads on X, videos on YouTube, posts on Substack. (We're not affiliated with any of those platforms.)

## How content maps onto LazyGiving

- **Each piece of content can be funded on its own.** A specific video, a specific post — supporters put money behind the individual items they value.
- **A creator groups pieces into a *contract*.** A content contract is just a [LazyGiving assurance contract](../shared/key-ideas/assurance-contracts.md) with a funding goal the creator sets. The creator decides which pieces go together and how much to ask for.
- **Supporters pledge toward the items they like.** "I'll chip in $5 for this video — as long as enough other people chip in to reach the goal." Reach the goal and the funds are released to the creator. Fall short and everyone is refunded. Nobody risks anything.
- **The creator owns the contract and receives the money.** A creator can start their own contracts. Anyone else can start one *for* a creator who hasn't shown up yet — but the funds wait for the creator, who [claims them by proving they control the account](get-your-content-funded.md). The money never goes to the third party.

Everything else — delegation, retroactive funding, cause portals — works exactly as it does on LazyGiving, because a content contract *is* a LazyGiving project. The rest is just how we've organized content items into LazyGiving-compatible projects.

## Why fund content this way?

Social-media content is a public good, and right now we pay for it almost entirely with ads. Ads reward whatever holds attention — which in practice means outrage, clickbait, and engagement bait — not the content people are actually glad they saw. Content Funding is a way to pay creators directly for work people value, without an advertiser in the middle deciding what gets made.

And because content contracts plug into the rest of the ecosystem, you can fund more than specific pieces. Through [Alignment](../alignment/index.md), pooled cause-money can flow to whole *kinds* of content that the ad model has never rewarded well: content that's funny, insightful, carefully researched, or [written to inform rather than inflame](../shared/use-case-walkthroughs/noninflammatory-content.md). If LazyGiving is a good way to fund public goods, this is what it looks like aimed at the public good of a healthier information commons.

## What can I do here?

- **[Get your content funded](get-your-content-funded.md)** — Claim your channel, group your content into a contract, and set a funding goal.
- **[Fund content you value](fund-content.md)** — Pledge toward the pieces you want to reward. Refunded if the goal isn't met.
