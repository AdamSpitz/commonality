# Get your content funded

## What this is

Any piece of content with a URL — an article, a YouTube video, a Substack post — can be registered in Content Funding and funded directly. If it meets the standards of a cause donors care about, money flows to you for producing it.

## Why you might want to do this

Ad revenue rewards engagement, not quality. Paywalls require readers to subscribe before they know if your work is worth it. Grants require applications and committees.

Content funding works differently: money is already pooled and waiting for content that meets certain standards. An AI evaluation service assesses whether your piece qualifies. If it does, you don't have to convince anyone — the funding flows automatically to content that passes.

This means you can write (or film, or publish) exactly what you believe is worth producing, without optimizing for outrage or building a following first. If what you make serves a funded cause, it gets rewarded.

## How it works

Donors pledge money to funding portals — pools of money tied to causes they care about. Some of those portals are configured to fund content: specifically, content that an AI evaluation service certifies as meeting a particular standard.

You submit your content to an evaluator. The evaluator (an AI service) assesses whether it meets the standard — say, "does this piece communicate a political perspective without antagonizing people who disagree?" or "is this open-source software documentation actually clear?" If it passes, an attestation is issued.

Donors whose portals trust that evaluator now see your content and can fund it — either directly or through delegates who make funding calls on their behalf.

**Claiming your channel:** If you're a recurring creator (YouTube channel, Substack, Twitter account), you can claim your channel so that funds addressed to your content come to you. The claim process verifies you control the account.

## Getting started

Open the **Creators** section on Content Funding and find your platform (Twitter/X, YouTube, Substack). Search for your channel. If it's already there (because someone submitted your content), claim it. If not, you can register it.

Once your channel is claimed, you can see what funding exists for content in your area, which AI evaluators are active, and what standards they assess. Then just produce the work — and submit pieces you want evaluated.

You don't need to build an audience on Commonality. The audience is already there, in the form of donors who've pledged to causes you might serve. Your job is to produce content worth funding.
