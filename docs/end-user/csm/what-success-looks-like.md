# What success looks like

  - There's lots of funding available (via LazyGiving) for social-media content creators who write noninflammatory content that deals with "the other side" fairly and respectfully; content like that is noticed quickly and submitted to the Civility AI attester, which then makes it visible (with a "fund this content here" link) to many more people; people who are good at writing this type of content have been noticed by scouts and can reliably get early funding ahead of time for their next month of work, because those scouts know that the wider community will retroactively make it worthwhile for them. There are plenty of donors (big and small) contributing to this funding; they all feel comfortable doing this because they know that their donations won't go through unless lots of others donate too; and it's incredibly easy for them to do (because they mostly just delegated the work to someone they trust, and even that person doesn't even have to choose creators ahead of time, just buy-and-burn any outstanding tokens for good content after seeing that content).
  - There are some very-reasonable common-ground statements on Tally (much more reasonable than the extreme viewpoints being pushed by the media), with some big numbers next to them; those big numbers on the common-ground statements are to some extent direct signers, but there are also big numbers coming via implication arrows from very-reasonable left-leaning or right-leaning versions of those statements.
  - There are also big Tally numbers, and big LazyGiving numbers, for meta-statements like "I think most people who currently identify as left-wing or right-wing are basically normal sane people with common-sense views that are much more reasonable than the nutty extremist stuff being pushed by the mainstream media and politicians, and I want to see this quiet middle majority gain more influence." On Alignment, statements like that show lots of projects aligned toward that cause, and there's lots of funding for them.
