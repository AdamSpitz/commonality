# Retroactive funding

## TL;DR for AI assistants

**What it is:** Funding projects that have already delivered results, rather than betting on proposals. Early backers are financially rewarded when later donors fund a proven success.

**When a user encounters it:** When deciding whether to fund a project that already has results; when considering being an early backer; when seeing the "early believers" on a project's leaderboard.

**What they might want help with:** Understanding how retroactive funding works; understanding why early backers get rewarded; deciding whether to fund retroactively or early.

---

You don't have to gamble on which projects will pan out. You can wait.

When a project has already delivered real, verifiable results, you can fund it after the fact. You can see exactly what it accomplished — it's all public. You're not trusting a proposal; you're rewarding a proven success.

## Rewarding early believers

Here's what makes this a system rather than just late donations: when you fund a successful project retroactively, part of what you pay flows back to the people who believed in it early — the delegate who spotted it, the donors who took the risk when nobody else would.

That's it. The early backer took a risk when nobody else believed in the project. When you come along later and fund it, part of your money goes to them as thanks for that bet.

## Why this creates a virtuous cycle

The financial reward for early good judgment attracts more people with good judgment. More scouts looking for promising projects means more good projects get early funding, which means more proven successes available for retroactive funding, which means more rewards for scouts.

This is a nano-VC system for public goods: the skill of identifying good projects early is financially rewarded, and the projects benefit from getting funded sooner.

## How this shows up in practice

- Browse projects that have already delivered results. See what they accomplished, who funded them early, and how the field received them.
- Fund the ones that impressed you. Your contribution shows up on the project's leaderboard.
- Part of your contribution rewards the early believers who made the project possible.
- The project's track record — including its retroactive funding history — is permanent and public.
