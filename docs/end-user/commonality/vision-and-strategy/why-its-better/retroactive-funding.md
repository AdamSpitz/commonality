# Retroactive funding

## The problem

You want to support work that actually helps. But judging proposals in advance is hard: a project might fail, be a scam, or simply be less capable than its pitch suggests. Traditional philanthropy often asks ordinary donors to make that judgment before there is much evidence.

## The simple answer

Fund things that have already worked.

If a project has delivered results — built the software, cleaned the river, run the program, published the research, or otherwise created visible value — donors can support that proven work with much less guesswork. This is retroactive funding: directing money toward results rather than promises.

## Who funds the work early?

The early role belongs to **scouts**: people with the context, taste, local knowledge, or courage to support promising work before it is obvious. They may use their own giving budget, or they may spend money that other donors have explicitly delegated to them.

In the redesigned Commonality flow, scouts are not promised upside. Their receipt is a record of a recoverable donation, not a tradable asset. When later donors decide that a project has delivered, those donations can reimburse early contributors **up to what they originally put in**. No interest, premium, bonus, or markup is part of the mechanism.

The loop is:

1. Scouts fund promising work early.
2. The project delivers something donors can inspect.
3. Retroactive donors donate to close the loop.
4. The system reimburses early contributors pro rata, at cost, until their recoverable donations are refilled.
5. Scouts can redeploy that giving capacity to the next project.

The point is capital velocity for public-good scouts, plus visible reputation for good judgment — not financial gain.

## What you see as a retroactive donor

From your perspective, the flow is simple:

1. Browse projects with trusted success attestations.
2. Review what the project accomplished and who vouched for it.
3. See the amount still needed to reimburse early contributors.
4. Donate to close the loop for projects you believe created real value.
5. Receive a permanent record of your donation and, if you choose, appear in public acknowledgements.

You are making a donation to sustain work that already happened and to refill the scouts who helped it happen early.

## Scout records and delegation

Commonality may show raw per-scout records such as:

> Scouted 14 projects, reimbursed on 9, still outstanding on 5.

or, for a specific project:

> Scouted US$200, reimbursed US$80, outstanding US$120.

These records are reputation signals. They help donors decide whose judgment they trust. They are not projections, yield claims, or platform guarantees.

Delegation is the natural complement: if you do not want to evaluate early projects yourself, you can delegate a giving budget to someone whose work and track record you respect. Suggested delegates are a UI convenience only. The protocol does not automatically route money to them; they receive responsibility over donated funds.

## Why this is better than prospective-only funding

- **Better evidence.** Donors can inspect delivered work instead of judging only promises.
- **Separate roles.** Scouts specialize in early judgment; retroactive donors specialize in supporting proven results.
- **Revolving capacity.** Reimbursement at cost lets early giving budgets be used again.
- **Visible reputation.** Good scout judgment compounds into trust, delegated budgets, and social proof.
- **Lower fraud risk.** It is harder to fake completed work than a polished proposal.

## Known limitations

Retroactive funding works best when results are legible and donors care enough to close the loop. Some work has impact that is delayed or hard to evaluate. A project can also remain unreimbursed if later donors do not show up. Those are real limitations, but they are clearer and safer than asking everyone to speculate in advance.

## How it fits with Commonality

- **Assurance contracts** help projects gather upfront commitments.
- **Success attestations** record who believes the work delivered.
- **Reimbursement records** show which early contributors are still waiting to be made whole.
- **Delegation** lets donors route budgets through people whose judgment they trust.

The whole story is: fund proven work, reimburse early contributors at cost, and make good judgment visible enough that more donors can rely on it.
