# Nearly costless to try-and-fail

It's nearly costless to try-and-fail, even for each individual little decision:

  - **Pledging** to an assurance contract risks nothing if others don't join — you get refunded if the threshold isn't met.
  - **Delegation** is revocable at any time — you're not committed to this delegate for the next four years. If you hand him a bit of money and he uses it for something you don't like, just turn off the spigot.
  - **Signing a statement** costs nothing.
  - **Starting a project** requires no upfront investment beyond clicking the button.

No need to be certain that even this one small thing will succeed, let alone that the entire system will succeed. There's no need to be confident that this is all a great idea before trying it out.
