# Civility

Civility is the home for finding, funding, and amplifying content that helps people understand each other instead of escalating conflict — bridge-building political writing, and discussion that informs rather than inflames.

It's a focused vertical built on top of [Content Funding](../content-funding/index.md). The funding mechanism is the same (supporters and cause pools fund content through assurance contracts), and the [AI-evaluator mechanism](../alignment/ai-evaluators.md) that decides which content qualifies is the same. What Civility adds is the *specific standard* and the evaluators tuned for it.

## The noninflammatory standard

The evaluators here judge whether a piece communicates a perspective in a way the other side can actually engage with: does it steelman the opposing view, avoid contempt, and resist tribal signaling? A strongly left-wing or strongly right-wing piece can pass — the question isn't whether it's centrist, but whether it invites engagement rather than defensiveness.

Different evaluators can apply stricter or looser versions of the standard, and calibrate for different audiences (e.g. "would a progressive who's willing to listen feel attacked by this?"). You choose which ones you trust.

## Beat agents

Some content can't be judged in isolation — whether a piece is fair often depends on the conversation around it. A **beat agent** is an evaluator that follows an ongoing slice of political discourse (a "beat") and judges content in that context, rather than treating each piece as self-contained. Beat agents are the first evaluators built for Civility, and the [Common Sense Majority](../csm/README.md) effort is a likely consumer of the discourse context they maintain.
