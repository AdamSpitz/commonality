# Won't this also be used for evil?

Ugh, probably.

This system is meant as a neutral tool, usable for helping people coordinate. It's certainly going to end up being used for purposes that I (as the creator of the system) would consider wrong.

I'm not really inclined to try to prevent that (e.g. by moderation/censorship). For one thing, any moderation mechanism would be a chokepoint that a hostile government could capture. A system that can censor the causes you dislike can also censor the causes you care about. That's a major part of the problem we're solving.

Still, does the use of this system for evil mean that I shouldn't build it?

## Every general-purpose tool gets used for evil

Email, encryption, the internet, cash, cars. The question isn't "can this be misused?" (yes, obviously) but "does it create more good than harm?" I think giving ordinary people better tools for funding public goods is net positive by a wide margin.

## The current system is also used for evil

Government spending is routinely captured by special interests, directed toward harmful policies, or used as leverage to coerce compliance on unrelated issues. Charitable organizations have been used for money laundering, tax evasion, and ideological capture. The comparison isn't "Commonality vs. a world without evil" — it's "Commonality vs. the status quo," and the status quo has plenty of its own problems. At least Commonality's problems are *visible*.

## The optimistic bet

I guess I'm expressing some optimism regarding humanity: I think people are more sane and good than our current systems might suggest, and if people in general had better ability to make public goods happen, the result would be overall more good than not.
