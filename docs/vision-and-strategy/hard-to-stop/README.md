# Hard to stop

"What if the government tries to stop it?"

Commonality is designed to be hard to stop, in various ways:

  - The core tech (blockchains, IPFS) is [censorship-resistant](./censorship-resistance.md). No bank accounts to freeze, no platform to deplatform, no org to shut down.
  - The system operates with [after-tax money you already control](./after-tax.md). This has the obvious disadvantage that you're eating a ~30-40% tax hit, but controlling money that's already in your hands is a hell of a lot easier than trying to influence what the government does with tax dollars.
  - And there are [game-theoretic reasons](./game-theory.md) to expect adoption across the political spectrum. That is: (a) you can't stop your political opponents from using this system to safeguard the funding for *their* pet projects, and (b) you can't stop them from someday regaining power so you may want to safeguard the funding for *your* pet projects.
