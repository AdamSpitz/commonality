# Independence / self-provision (candidate movement)

**Status: founder-facing strategy, not public.** This is the "shift power away from the existing mainstream system" material that was extracted from Commonality's public `vision-and-strategy/` docs (see [the cofounder discussion that motivated it](../../end-user/commonality/vision-and-strategy/README.md) for context). It's shelved here as a candidate for a *future* movement site, separate from Commonality.

## Why it's its own thing

Commonality is the calm, broadly-palatable thesis: *public-goods funding can get dramatically better, and here's the family of tools that do it.* Its audience is curious adopters, builders, and organizations.

This material is a different beast — hotter, more politically charged, and aimed at a different audience:

> You don't have to wait for the system's permission, win the next election, or survive the next budget cut. You can build independent, unstoppable funding capacity for the things your community cares about — and merely being *able* to changes the power dynamic.

It's explicitly cross-partisan: mutual-aid organizers on the left, defunded-program and local-control advocates on the right. Its register is empowerment and resilience, not "here's a neat improvement." Leaving it inside Commonality dragged the whole hub toward "anti-government movement," which undercuts Commonality's neutrality and bleeds charge onto the deliberately-neutral product sites. So it lives here until we decide whether to operate it as a real movement site (the way CSM is).

Like CSM, it would be a *movement framing layered on the same substrate* — it uses LazyGiving / Alignment for the actual funding.

## What's here

The core arguments, all about independence from centralized funding control:

  - **[Censorship resistance](./censorship-resistance.md)** — no chokepoints for a hostile government: no bank accounts to freeze, no platform to ban, no org to shut down.
  - **[After-tax dollars](./after-tax.md)** — handling the obvious objection that you're paying with after-tax money instead of getting the government to pay.
  - **[Credible threats](./credible-threat.md)** — a strike fund for public goods: visibly being *able* to fund something yourself shifts the negotiation, even if you never spend a dime.
  - **[Game theory](./game-theory.md)** — why adoption is rational across the political spectrum even without enthusiasm: defunding insurance, plus competitive pressure once the other side adopts.
  - **[Easier than politics](./easier-than-politics.md)** — many "political" problems are really just funding problems; you can start solving them today instead of organizing a movement or winning an election.
  - **[More-local government](./local-government.md)** — voluntary public-goods funding has a structural gravity toward localism, which over time may route power around higher levels of government.

## Related: the localism-movement spec

[`specs/product/localism-movement.md`](/specs/product/localism-movement.md) already contemplates a *localism / civic self-funding* domain built on this thesis, and recommends "build a thin kernel now, defer the full domain until the prerequisite primitives exist." Localism is one sub-theme of the broader independence/self-provision material collected here (`local-government.md` is the essay that spec is built around). If this graduates into a real site, that spec and this folder should be reconciled — including the naming question (localism is narrower than the deeper claim of *ad hoc regional cooperation without permanent regional government*).

## The open question

A site is a promise to *operate* something, not just host docs. The gate is: do we actually want to run an independence/self-provision movement, or just keep the argument shelved? If we run it, this folder becomes the seed of its docs. If not, it stays here as reference.
