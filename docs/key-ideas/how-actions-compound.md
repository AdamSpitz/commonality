## TL;DR for AI assistants

**What it is:** An explanation of how each individual action on the platform doesn't just benefit the person taking it — it makes the system work better for everyone else. The cumulative effect is a funding ecosystem that gets faster, smarter, and more efficient over time.

**When a user encounters it:** When onboarding; when deciding whether to participate; when trying to explain the system to someone else.

**What they might want help with:** Understanding the bigger picture; seeing how their small action fits into the ecosystem; articulating the value of the system to others.

Every action you take on Commonality does something useful for you right away. But it also makes the system work better for everyone else. Here's how.

## What each action does for the ecosystem

**Delegating your funding** doesn't just take work off your plate — it makes projects easier to fund. Every dollar sitting in a delegate's pool is a dollar that a project creator doesn't need to fundraise for individually. Instead of convincing a thousand donors one by one, a project creator needs to convince a handful of delegates who already have pooled funds earmarked for that cause. Your laziness is, genuinely, a contribution.

**Funding a project retroactively** doesn't just reward a proven success — it de-risks scouting. Scouts and delegates who fund projects early are taking a bet: they believe the project will deliver, but they're not sure anyone will reimburse them later. Every time you fund something that already worked, by buying a token from a scout who took a risk by buying it earlier — you prove that bet pays off. That puts money back in the hands of the scouts, which makes them more willing to fund the *next* promising project early, which means good projects get funded faster.

**Pledging to an assurance contract** doesn't just move the progress bar — it changes other people's calculations. Every pledge makes the threshold more likely to be met, which makes other potential pledgers more willing to commit. Visible progress attracts more progress. ## The emergent picture

Zoom out, and the individual actions add up to something none of them could accomplish alone:

- A project creator posts a project. A scout spots it early and funds it with delegated money, because she trusts that retroactive funders will reimburse her later.
- The project delivers. Retroactive funders see the proven results and fund it after the fact, rewarding the scout.
- The scout's track record improves, attracting more delegators. She has more funds to deploy, and she's learned what works.
- Next time, a similar project gets funded even faster — the scout is more confident, has more capital, and the retroactive funders have a pattern to follow.

The steady state is a system where good projects get funded almost immediately by scouts who aren't worried about reimbursement, because the track record of retroactive funding gives them confidence. Donors who don't have time to evaluate projects just delegate and check in occasionally. Attesters keep the signal-to-noise ratio manageable. And the whole thing runs without anyone needing to organize it.

Each action you take greases one of these wheels. None of them is world-changing on its own. But they're all real, they all compound, and together they squeeze the inefficiency out of how we fund the things we care about.
