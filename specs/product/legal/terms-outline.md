# Terms outline (draft for counsel)

Not legal advice and not production terms. This is a plain-language checklist for the eventual Terms of Service / Acceptable Use Policy.

## Acceptable-use prohibitions

Users may not use Commonality-operated sites, APIs, sponsored-gas endpoints, or other services to:

- launder money or disguise the source, ownership, or destination of funds;
- commit fraud, use stolen payment instruments, or cash out proceeds from stolen-card or account-takeover activity;
- evade sanctions, terrorist-financing rules, export controls, or the screening/KYC checks of licensed fiat on-ramp/off-ramp providers;
- fund, solicit, or promote illegal projects or activities;
- misrepresent project affiliation, recipient identity, or the purpose/use of funds;
- abuse sponsored gas, on-ramp session endpoints, APIs, or other subsidized infrastructure.

## Enforcement posture for operated services

For sites and services we operate, reserve the right to hide, delist, refuse sponsored gas, disable on-ramp session creation, rate-limit, or otherwise stop facilitating projects/addresses when we have reason to believe they violate the acceptable-use policy or create sanctions, fraud, money-laundering, or platform-abuse risk.

This does **not** imply custody or unilateral control over the underlying contracts: onchain contracts remain non-custodial and may be accessible through other interfaces.

## Marketing constraint

Do not market Commonality as anonymous, KYC-free, or a way to bypass licensed fiat on/off-ramp compliance. The intended posture is the opposite: Commonality does not custody or transmit funds, and fiat edges remain with licensed providers that run their own fraud, sanctions, and KYC controls.
