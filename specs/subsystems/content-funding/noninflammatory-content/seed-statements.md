# Seed Statements for Noninflammatory Content

Concrete statements to seed the conceptspace for the noninflammatory content use case. These are designed to be signed by real people expressing what they actually value, in language natural to their political community.

The key design principle: **don't write centrist statements that nobody would actually sign.** Write statements that people from specific communities would sign because it reflects something they genuinely want. The implication attester then discovers the connections between them.


## Hub statement

This is the abstract statement that the implication attester should link the others to. Most people won't sign this directly — they'll sign the more specific statements below, and the implication graph connects them.

> **"Content that communicates political perspectives without antagonizing people who disagree deserves financial support."**


## Bridge statements

Statements that many people across the spectrum might sign. These are the most natural connection points.

> **"I want the political content I consume to inform me, not inflame me."**

> **"Political ideas are more persuasive when they're presented without contempt for the people who currently disagree."**

> **"If someone holds views I disagree with, I'd rather read a version that makes their best case than a version designed to make me angry at them."**


## Left-coded statements

Statements that progressives would sign, expressed in terms natural to the left. These are *not* centrist concessions — they're things a committed progressive would genuinely endorse.

> **"Progressive ideas are strong enough to be communicated without demonizing conservatives."**

The left doesn't need to resort to caricature. If the ideas are good, they can be presented in a way that lets people evaluate them on the merits.

> **"I want conservative people in my life to be able to engage with progressive content without feeling attacked."**

Many left-leaning people have conservative family members, coworkers, or neighbors. Content that lets them share progressive ideas without starting a fight is genuinely valuable to them.

> **"Left-wing media that makes me feel righteous but alienates everyone who disagrees with me is not actually helping my cause."**

This is the self-aware progressive who recognizes the difference between catharsis and persuasion.

> **"Progressives should be able to make their case to a skeptical audience, not just preach to the choir."**


## Right-coded statements

Statements that conservatives would sign, expressed in terms natural to the right. Same principle — these reflect things a committed conservative would genuinely endorse.

> **"Conservative ideas deserve a hearing that isn't poisoned by liberal media framing them as bigotry."**

This speaks to a real conservative frustration: that their ideas are strawmanned or presented in the worst possible light by mainstream media. Content that presents conservative ideas fairly is genuinely what they want.

> **"I want to be able to share my political views with liberal friends and family without being written off as a bad person."**

The mirror of the left-coded family statement. Many conservatives feel that expressing their views leads to social punishment rather than engagement.

> **"Right-wing media that just dunks on the left is entertaining but it's not convincing anyone who doesn't already agree."**

The self-aware conservative who recognizes the difference between rallying the base and actually persuading.

> **"If conservative ideas are good, we should be able to explain them in a way that a fair-minded liberal would at least understand, even if they still disagree."**


## Expected implication structure

The implication attester should discover a structure roughly like this (arrows = "implies"):

```
  "Progressive ideas are strong enough to be         "Conservative ideas deserve a hearing
   communicated without demonizing conservatives"      that isn't poisoned by liberal media..."
                    \                                          /
                     v                                        v
        "Political ideas are more persuasive when they're presented
         without contempt for the people who currently disagree"
                                    |
                                    v
        "Content that communicates political perspectives without
         antagonizing people who disagree deserves financial support"
```

The key insight: a progressive who signs "Progressive ideas are strong enough to be communicated without demonizing conservatives" and a conservative who signs "Conservative ideas deserve a hearing that isn't poisoned by liberal media framing them as bigotry" end up implicitly supporting the same hub statement — and therefore implicitly supporting each other's noninflammatory content. Neither side had to sign the other's statement or even know about it.

This is the organic coalition in action.


## Statements that we should NOT seed

Some things to explicitly avoid:

- **"Both sides are equally bad"** — Nobody actually believes this and it's a straw centrist position.
- **"We should all just be nicer to each other"** — Too vague, too preachy, and doesn't describe anything fundable.
- **"I'm socially liberal but fiscally conservative"** — This is a political identity, not a statement about content quality.
- **"The truth is somewhere in the middle"** — The system isn't about centrism; it's about communicating *any* perspective without being inflammatory. A strongly left-wing post can be noninflammatory.
