# No need to compromise

In traditional collective action, you lose your individuality the moment you join a coalition. The price of coordination is compromise: you sign statements you don't fully believe, you back candidates who half-represent you, you accept leaders you didn't choose. Every step of the pipeline forces you to converge with the crowd.

Commonality eliminates each of these ugh moments:

  - **Signing a statement** no longer means finding the most-popular version that "sorta vaguely" captures what you think. Sign exactly what you believe. Implication attestations will connect you with everyone who said something similar — without requiring anyone to coordinate on wording first.
  - **Trusting attesters** is an individual choice, not a collective vote. You don't have to accept whatever "official" attester your group decided on. If you trust Alice and your neighbour trusts Bob, that's fine — each person just trusts whoever they trust. And if an attester goes bad, you can individually stop trusting them; no need to organize a vote of no confidence.
  - **Delegating** is a personal decision, not a district-wide election. You don't have to share a delegate with thousands of strangers who have different priorities. Delegate $X to Alice for cause C and $Y to Bob for cause D — no need to find one person who represents you on everything.
  - **Filtering projects for alignment** is something each person controls individually. No need to argue over who gets to be the gatekeeper. You choose whose alignment-attestations you trust; your neighbour chooses differently. No need to agree.
  - **There's no centralized treasury** requiring collective agreement to create, govern, or spend. Individuals pledge directly to specific projects; no committee decides how pooled funds get allocated.

The common thread: every step that used to require collective agreement is now an individual decision. You participate on exactly the terms you want, without waiting for anyone's permission.
