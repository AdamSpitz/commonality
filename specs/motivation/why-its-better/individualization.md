# Individualization

The core insight: preserving individual preferences further into the pipeline produces better collective outcomes. Traditional collective action aggregates early — contributions are pooled into a central entity (treasury, vote, committee) and individuality is lost at that step. Commonality pushes aggregation as late as possible, using the full richness of individual signals to find genuine common ground.

The result is better signal, better matching, and better accountability at each step:

  - **Signing a statement:** When everyone says exactly what they think rather than rallying around a compromise, the system has much richer data about what people actually want. Implication attestations can discover genuine overlap between groups — including groups that didn't know they agreed — that a single compromised statement would never reveal.
  - **Trusting attesters individually:** Different people have expertise in different domains. Letting each person choose their own attesters means signals are filtered by someone appropriate for the specific domain and context. Competing attesters also have ongoing incentive to produce quality work; a single "official" attester faces no such pressure.
  - **Per-cause, per-person delegation:** A delegate who represents you specifically on a specific cause will make better decisions for you than a generalist representing your whole district on all issues. And since delegation is revocable at any time, delegates are accountable continuously — not just at election time.
  - **Distributed alignment-attestation:** Multiple independent attesters create redundancy and check each other. No single point of failure or capture. If one attester goes rogue, only the people who trusted that attester are affected; the rest of the system is unharmed.
  - **No centralized treasury:** Funds flow to the projects individuals actually want to fund, without passing through a committee. That committee step introduces both overhead and political gamesmanship; removing it means more resources reach their intended destination.
